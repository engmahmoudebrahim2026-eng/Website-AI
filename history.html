1943 – 1956

The Founding Era

The dream of thinking machines ignites. Mathematicians, logicians, and engineers lay the theoretical foundations that will define the field for decades.

1943
McCulloch-Pitts Neuron

First mathematical model of a biological neuron — the seed from which all neural networks would grow.

1950
The Turing Test

Alan Turing asks "Can machines think?" and proposes the Imitation Game as a test of machine intelligence.

1956
Dartmouth Conference — AI is Born

John McCarthy coins "Artificial Intelligence." The field is officially founded as a scientific discipline.

1956 – 1974

The Golden Age

Immense optimism. Early AI programs play checkers, solve algebra, prove theorems. Bold predictions of human-level AI within 20 years.

1957Perceptron invented — first trainable neural network
1959Arthur Samuel coins "Machine Learning"
1966ELIZA — first chatbot simulating conversation
1969Shakey the Robot — first mobile AI robot
1974 – 1980

The First AI Winter

Computational limits, insufficient data, and algorithmic failures make grand promises impossible. Funding collapses worldwide.

"AI has been consistently overpromised and underdelivered." — 1970s scientific community

1973Lighthill Report devastates UK AI funding
1974US Congress cuts DARPA AI budget by 80%
1980s

The Expert Systems Boom

AI revives through commercial applications. Rule-based "expert systems" encode human domain knowledge — and businesses actually pay for them.

1980
XCON — First Commercial AI Hit

Digital Equipment Corp's expert system saved $40M/year. Proved AI's business value.

1981
Japan's $850M Fifth Generation Project

Sparked a global AI investment race. US and UK immediately launched competing programs.

1986
Backpropagation Breakthrough

Hinton, Rumelhart & Williams make training deep neural networks practical — echoing for decades.

2023 — The AI Arms Race

GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama flood the market. $100B+ invested. Every major tech company races to integrate AI.

2024 — Nobel Prizes for AI

Geoffrey Hinton (Physics) and Demis Hassabis (Chemistry) win Nobel Prizes — cementing AI in scientific history.

2025 — Agentic AI Emerges

AI agents autonomously browse, code, and complete multi-step tasks. A new paradigm beyond conversation.
